## 📖 Overview

We hate ads. This isn't an emotional statement — it's a structural fact.

For the past 25 years, digital advertising has been built on a fundamental conflict: the user's goal and the advertiser's goal are inherently opposed. The result? A distorted value exchange where we pay money to *remove* ads. 75% of iOS users opt out of tracking. 63% of U.S. adults say ads within AI search results erode their trust.

But in 2026, the arrival of the AI era is shaking this structure to its core.

Google, OpenAI, Anthropic, Perplexity, Meta, Microsoft, Amazon — seven companies have arrived at radically different answers to the same question about AI and advertising. Some are embracing ads, some are rejecting them outright, and one tried and retreated. The smartest people in the world are making opposite bets on the same problem.

**This book dissects the structure of that divide, moves beyond the binary of "should we include ads or not," and arrives at a different question entirely: "Can we redesign advertising into something users actually welcome?"**

## 🏗️ Structure

The book comprises 9 chapters.

| Ch. | Title | Content |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | The Original Sin of Advertising — Why We Hate Ads | 25 years of "intrusion" by design, the distorted value exchange of subscriptions, the "quiet rebellion" of ad blockers — and the structural reason ads won't die |
| 2 | The End of Search, the Beginning of Conversation | From keyword search to conversational decision engines. Google's dual-layer AI ad strategy, the internal warning from DeepMind, and the shift from "What" to "Why" |
| 3 | Seven Companies, Seven Choices — The Polarization | OpenAI's reversal, Anthropic's unequivocal refusal, Perplexity's retreat, and three distinct approaches from Meta, Microsoft, and Amazon. A positioning matrix of all seven |
| 4 | The Trust Paradox — More Transparency, Less Trust | The uncomfortable truth Perplexity revealed. The structural mechanics of "users start doubting everything." How Anthropic proved the value of absence |
| 5 | Can Advertising Work as "Proposal"? — Five Conditions | The design principles of value exchange, Google Direct Offers, Microsoft Copilot's evidence (CTR +73%), and the economic reality behind Sam Altman's reversal |
| 6 | Personal Intelligence — The Boundary Between Ultimate Personalization and Privacy | When AI knows you better than you know yourself. Google's guardrail design, Meta's "no opt-out" shock, and EU AI Act Article 50 |
| 7 | Agentic Commerce — When AI Completes the Purchase | Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP), agentic checkout, the evolution of ads from "showing" to "executing," and the risk of disintermediation |
| 8 | The End of SEO — The Age of Probabilistic Visibility | SparkToro/Gumshoe.ai's striking experiment, Google Patent US12536233B1, WebMCP, content optimization as "citation fuel," and Wayfair's practice |
| 9 | Can Trust Survive the Introduction of Ads? — Conclusion | A four-variable equation, three scenarios for 2030, and the shift from "should we include ads?" to "can we redesign them into something welcome?" |
